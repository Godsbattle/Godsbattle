# GitHub Profile Refresh Design

## Objective

Present Christian Obanaka accurately and efficiently on GitHub. The profile should make clear that futures trading is his primary profession, community and affiliate management is his trading-industry career, and creating tools is how he solves practical problems for himself and other traders.

`trackmyprop` is Christian's flagship long-term product, but the profile must not imply that building trading software is his entire career or that every future project will be trading software.

## Positioning hierarchy

1. Futures trader.
2. Community and affiliate manager working with prop firms, trading companies, and brands.
3. Creator of practical tools and resources for traders, including software, Notion systems, journals, templates, and educational material.
4. `trackmyprop` as the current flagship product and serious long-term focus.
5. GodsBattle as evidence of Christian's established technology-content background.

## Tone and presentation

- Clear, grounded, and professional.
- Written in the first person.
- Focused on real work and verifiable outcomes rather than aspirational labels.
- Concise enough to scan without becoming a badge wall or product advertisement.
- No GitHub statistics widgets, decorative technology badges, or generic claims about passion.

## Current focus

### trackmyprop

[trackmyprop](https://trackmyprop.app) is my flagship long-term product: desktop software for prop-firm traders who need a better way to manage accounts, journal trades, follow challenge rules, control risk, and understand their performance.

It began as a solution for my own trading workflow and is being developed for other traders facing the same problems.

Most development happens in a private monorepo. Public beta releases are available in [trackmyprop-desktop-releases](https://github.com/Godsbattle/trackmyprop-desktop-releases).

## What I do

- Create trading journals, Notion templates, software, and other useful resources.
